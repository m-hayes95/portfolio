Research

As our goal of the project it to create an energetic experience, it was crucial that we took the research stage seriously and do as much as possible in the given time to prepare and ensure we were creating an experience that was meeting this criteria.

The image to the left shows some of the research notes on creating an energetic experience 

Some of our key takeaways:

  • Music is extremely important - Increase of tempo from beginning to end 
  • Colour palette should be lively colours
  • Make mechanics intuitive, simple and fast to acquire 
  • Challenge of reducing player fatigue and cognitive load
  • Balance of joy and arousal needed to prevent the player feeling stress 

User Research: Demographic

We created a questionnaire for members of our school and recorded their answers. We then created personas from them for the most common themes we found.

User Research: Archetypes

Archetypes are derived from Personas. This allowed us to identify our key users, keeping them in mind whilst creating the game and helping us maintain a focused development time.

Testing

Testing feedback results organised into tasks to act on (Sorted by priority & time)

We found that testing was a huge part of creating an enjoyable experience for VR. Although we would have liked to have had more participants test the game and this will be something that we would look to improve significantly in future projects.

It allowed us to realise fundamental issues the gameplay had allowed us to tweak them for a better experience. For example, through testing we found the targets were too close in 3d space to the player which created an uncomfortable feeling for some players. 

Improvements

There are many improvements that can be made for this game. At this point I believe we have created a good base, with an enjoyable shooting mechanic. Although some polish is needed to make it feel even more impactful.

The main purpose of the game is to make the player feel a sense of excitement and increase their energy levels throughout the play session. I believe that the music needs to be revised to make it more faster paced by increasing the BMP. We tried to work with the artist by increasing this as much as possible whilst maintaining the quality of the music, I don't feel we have gone quite far enough, so the choice of song will need to be revised.

In terms of gameplay, our current main mechanic for increasing the intensity throughout the level is by matching the target’s spawn rate with the beat which increases overtime. This should make the game feel more faced paced over the song, but it's not quite meeting that feeling. Perhaps a more exaggerated spawn rate is required, which could possibly lead to player fatigue or sickness. Alternatively, we could introduce other mechanics that lead to progression throughout the level, perhaps a mechanic such as evolving guns to more powerful versions with more cool effects would help us achieve our gameplay goals. 

For the final target we wanted to implement a visual effect that was more fun than just a single target exploding. This will require more brainstorming.

Project Thoughts

Overall, I believe the project was run well and we developed good team chemistry throughout. It was a fun learning experience creating a VR game for the first time.

It was interesting to see how the game worlds and target spawn area size affect how far the player had to turn. If the area was too large, it would make the player feel sickness or fatigue after a short amount of time. This experience showed me how much testing of features and their effects on players is crucial to make an enjoyable experience. 

Unfortunately, I don't quite think we hit all our intended goals by the end of the project. After playtesting the game myself and watching others play, the gameplay does not elevate the excitement levels enough and after multiple playthroughs it becomes quite tedious.
To remedy this I believe we need to create more development throughout the experience. It's current state is too simple and only progresses slightly in tempo throughout as more targets spawn. The current mechanics in place to create this excitement, increased spawn rate over time and targets de-spawning if not shot in time are not enough.

Perhaps having the weapons upgrade after destroying a certain amount of targets, or creating more excitement / agency for the targets would improve the overall enjoyability of the experience. The ending mechanic also needs more revisions and testing to become viable.

Besides the previously mentioned problems and need for the overall project's need for polishing there are some positive aspects to take home. For the team's first VR project, I feel we were close to achieving a good user experience as we took notes from industry professionals (thanks to the Liminal VR team and GDC vault), and I do believe we met our 2nd goal of creating a VR experience that is suitable for new VR players. Through testing the game on players who have little experience in VR, they were able to quickly identify and learn the new mechanics after a short amount of time, although the playtesting sample size was quite small so this data may be unreliable.
